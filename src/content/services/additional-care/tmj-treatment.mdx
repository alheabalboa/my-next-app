## What to expect

We examine the joint, bite, and muscles, then build a plan that may include a custom appliance, exercises, and referrals when needed.

## Benefits of TMJ treatment

- **Relief from chronic pain** — addresses daily jaw soreness, facial pain, and tension headaches caused by TMJ disorders.
- **Improved jaw function** — restores comfortable opening and closing of the mouth, making eating and speaking easier.
- **Prevents further damage** — early intervention stops progressive joint deterioration and muscle strain.
- **Better sleep and less clenching** — reduces nighttime teeth grinding (bruxism) that disrupts sleep and damages teeth.

## When to book

- Jaw pain, clicking, or locking
- Morning headaches or sore jaw muscles
- You grind or clench your teeth at night
- Your bite feels 'off' or teeth are wearing unevenly

## Frequently asked questions

<details open>
<summary>What are the signs I might need TMJ treatment?</summary>

Common symptoms include jaw pain, clicking or popping sounds, headaches, earaches, facial tension, and difficulty opening or closing your mouth.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How is TMJ disorder diagnosed?</summary>

We assess your symptoms, jaw movement, and bite alignment. Imaging like X-rays or CBCT scans may be recommended to confirm the diagnosis.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What treatment options are available?</summary>

Treatments may include bite splints, nightguards, lifestyle modifications, physical therapy, and dental adjustments or restorations to balance your bite.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How long does it take to feel better?</summary>

Many patients notice improvement within a few weeks of starting treatment, though timelines vary depending on the severity of the disorder.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can TMJ treatment prevent teeth grinding?</summary>

Yes. Many TMJ treatment plans include nightguards or splints that reduce grinding and clenching, protecting your teeth and jaw.

</details>
