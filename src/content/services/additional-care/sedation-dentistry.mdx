## What to expect

We review your medical history, discuss which option fits, and make sure you're comfortable at every step. An escort home is required for oral sedation.

## Benefits of sedation dentistry

- **Reduces dental anxiety** — sedation makes it easier to get the care you need without stress or panic.
- **Pain-free procedures** — sedation minimizes discomfort, making treatments feel quicker and more tolerable — ideal for sensitive teeth or low pain tolerance.
- **Quicker treatment time** — with a calm, still patient, your dentist works more efficiently — often meaning fewer appointments.
- **Ideal for complex or lengthy procedures** — whether it's a root canal, extraction, or multiple procedures in one session, sedation makes it manageable.

## When to book

- Dental visits cause real anxiety, not just mild nerves
- A procedure is long and you'd rather rest through it
- A strong gag reflex makes routine care hard
- Previous experiences have made dental work difficult

## Frequently asked questions

<details open>
<summary>Is sedation dentistry safe?</summary>

Yes — sedation dentistry is extremely safe when administered by a qualified dentist. Safety and comfort are top priorities at Rundlehorn Smiles Dental.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Will I be asleep during sedation?</summary>

Most patients remain awake but feel deeply relaxed and often remember very little of the procedure. Various levels of sedation are available to match your needs.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How long does sedation last?</summary>

Duration depends on the type used. Oral sedation can last several hours, while nitrous oxide typically wears off within minutes after the procedure.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I drive home after sedation?</summary>

If you receive oral sedation, you'll need someone to drive you home. Nitrous oxide effects wear off rapidly and may allow you to drive yourself.

</details>
