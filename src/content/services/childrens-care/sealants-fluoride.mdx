## What to expect

Sealants are painted onto the grooves of back teeth and cured with light — no drilling. Fluoride varnish is brushed on in under a minute.

## Benefits of sealants & fluoride

- **Shields teeth from cavities** — sealants block food and bacteria from settling into deep grooves, while fluoride strengthens enamel against acid attacks.
- **Quick and painless prevention** — fast, non-invasive, and completely comfortable — ideal for kids, teens, and adults alike.
- **Saves time and money on future dental work** — preventing cavities upfront helps avoid more costly treatments like fillings, crowns, or root canals.
- **Ideal for children and teens** — especially effective for young patients with developing teeth and habits.

## When to book

- Your child's first adult molars have come in
- They've had cavities before
- Deep grooves are visible on the back teeth
- They're at higher cavity risk due to diet or hygiene habits

## Frequently asked questions

<details open>
<summary>What age should my child get dental sealants?</summary>

Most children should get sealants as soon as their permanent molars come in — typically between ages 6 and 12.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How long do sealants last?</summary>

Sealants can protect teeth for up to 5–10 years, though we'll check them regularly and reapply if necessary.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is fluoride treatment safe?</summary>

Yes — professional fluoride treatments are safe and effective for children and adults when applied in the proper dose by a trained professional.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can adults get sealants too?</summary>

Absolutely. While sealants are most common for children, adults without fillings in their molars may also benefit from added protection.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How often should I get fluoride treatments?</summary>

Depending on your risk of decay, fluoride treatments may be recommended every 3, 6, or 12 months to maintain strong enamel.

</details>
